Our History

From a guest lecture in Amsterdam to hosting international olympiad finals — KBI Netherlands has been building practical emerging-technology pathways in the Netherlands since 2019.

How it started

How it started

KBI Netherlands was established in 2019, growing from a relationship between KBI’s founding team and educators at Hogeschool InHolland in Amsterdam. What began as academic collaboration became one of KBI’s most active European presences — and the host chapter for ETO Western 2026.

2019

KBI Netherlands chapter established. Partnership formed with Hogeschool InHolland, Amsterdam.

2020

Guest lecture series at InHolland on blockchain, tokenomics, and ethics. Four Dutch teams compete at IBCOL 2020 — InHolland wins Bronze for a blockchain + IoT supply chain solution. The winning team goes on to explore a proof-of-concept pilot with Philips.

2021

Two Dutch teams compete at IBCOL 2021 in Dhaka. Netherlands educators join the official adjudication panel for the first time.

2022

Two Dutch teams compete at IBCOL 2022. Netherlands educators serve on the adjudication panel again.

2023

InHolland hosts IBCOL 2023 Finals at Science Museum NEMO, Amsterdam — part of DigiQuest 2023. First time an international KBI finals is hosted in the Netherlands.

2024

InHolland hosts ETO 2024 at DigiQuest 2024 — combining IBCOL and IDSOL finals. KBI Netherlands becomes the host chapter for the combined international finals.

2026

ETO Western 2026 at InHolland University of Applied Sciences, 8–9 October — showcasing and celebrating outstanding work in BCTT, DSAI, and QCIT. Open to participants worldwide.
