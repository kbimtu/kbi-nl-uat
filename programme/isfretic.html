ISFRETIC

A new standard for emerging-technology competence

Not what courses you attended. What you can actually do. ISFRETIC — the International Standards Framework of Reference for Emerging Technologies Innovation and Competency — is KBI's independent certification system for blockchain and trust technologies, data science and AI, and quantum computing. It is being built carefully, with one aim: that when someone holds an ISFRETIC credential, people know what it means.

What It Is

Competence, not attendance

Most credentials tell you what someone studied. ISFRETIC tells you what someone can do. It is modelled on a six-level scale, from A1 (Awareness) through to C2 (Expert Leadership), with clear descriptors for each level. The framework covers individuals, teams, and organisations — from first-step learners to advanced practitioners and system owners.

Fields

Three fields, one framework

BCTT — Blockchain and Trust Technologies

Distributed ledgers, smart contracts, digital identity, tokenisation, and governance.

DSAI — Data Science and Artificial Intelligence

Data, models, pipelines, and AI systems in real use — including governance and accountability.

QCIT — Quantum Computing and Information Technologies

Quantum information, quantum computing, and post-quantum readiness for organisations.

Levels

Six levels. One clear ladder.

A1
Awareness
You understand the basics and can explain why it matters.
A2
Explorer
You can follow structured workflows with guidance.
B1
Practitioner
You work semi-independently on bounded tasks.
B2
Independent Practitioner
You can take a problem and build a working solution.
C1
Advanced Practitioner
You design and lead complex work in the field.
C2
Expert Leader
You shape practice and contribute to standards.

A short ISFRETIC Core module provides a common foundation in informatics, cybersecurity basics, ethics, systems thinking, and communication for all fields and levels.

Who and What It Covers

People, teams, and systems

  • Individuals — Through exams, practical assessments, and portfolio review.
  • Teams — Through endorsements that recognise collective capability.
  • Organisations and projects — Through assurance marks that focus on the systems themselves.

These are not developer certificates in the narrow sense. They are signals about whether you, your team, or your system can be trusted to do the work you claim.

Independence

Independent by design

ISFRETIC is run as an independent standards body, structurally separate from KBI's training and commercial work. Exam content, question banks, and pass thresholds are governed by Scheme Committees made up of domain experts, academic representatives, employer voices, and elected Certified Members. Training programmes — including those offered by KBI — do not control the exams. That separation is deliberate. A credential only means something if the body that issues it is not also selling the answer key.
