Socratic Circles

Structured sessions where teams test ideas through questioning and critique — sharpening logic, assumptions, and project foundations before competitions and presentations.

About

Thinking through questions, not slides

A Socratic Circle is not a lecture and not a pitch. It is a facilitated session where a team presents its thinking — and other participants ask questions designed to expose assumptions, test logic, and strengthen the underlying argument. The format is especially valuable before major competitions, project reviews, and public presentations, when teams need honest feedback rather than encouragement alone.

How It Works

A typical session

  • A team presents its project, research question, or competition approach in a fixed time.
  • Participants ask questions — no speeches, no premature solutions.
  • A facilitator keeps the discussion rigorous, respectful, and on track.
  • The team leaves with clearer priorities and stronger foundations for the work ahead.
